PythonAsia 2026 in Manila: Everything You Need to Know About the Venue, Transport, Accommodations, and Local Eats

Everything you need to know about the venue, transport, accommodations, and local eats.

📍 Venue

De La Salle University (DLSU)
2401 Taft Avenue, Malate, Manila, 1004 Metro Manila

All talks, workshops, and sprints will be held inside the DLSU campus. The venue is easily reachable via LRT-1 (Vito Cruz Station).

🏫 High-Level Guide to Venue Facilities

  • Entrance Gate: Gate 2: North Gate – First point of contact for attendees
  • Registration Area & Help Desk: Pardo Hall Function Lobby, Henry Sy Bldg. (5th Flr.) – Assistance, directions, and inquiries
  • Main Hall: Teresa Yuchengco Auditorium (7th–9th Flr., Bldg. 10) – Keynote & plenary sessions
  • Secondary Hall: Pardo Hall Multi-Purpose Room, Henry Sy Bldg. (5th Flr.) – Additional plenary sessions & talks
  • Breakout / Workshop Rooms:
    • Breakout Room 1: Teresa Yuchengco Hall, Y507 (5th Flr.)
    • Breakout Room 2: Teresa Yuchengco Hall, Y508 (5th Flr.)
    • Breakout Room 3: Teresa Yuchengco Hall, Y509 (5th Flr.)
  • Poster Session: TBA
  • Sponsor Area: Pardo Hall Function Lobby, Henry Sy Bldg. (5th Flr.) – Networking & sponsor booths
  • Networking Area: The Verdure Function Lobby, Henry Sy Bldg. (4th Flr.) – Open space with cocktail tables designed for community networking
  • Dining Hall / Prayer Room: The Verdure Multi-Purpose Room, Henry Sy Bldg. (4th Flr.) – Meals and general use
  • Prayer Rooms for Muslim Attendees: Breakout Rooms 3 & 5, The Verdure Multi-Purpose Room (4th Flr.)
    • A breakout room in the Dining Hall is designated for prayer
    • Available mainly during lunch and snack breaks, with quiet hours suitable for prayer
    • Conveniently located near the 4th-floor bathrooms
    • Visit the Help Desk for assistance or directions

🗒️ Road Awareness Tips

  • Traffic culture is unique; pedestrian lanes and stop signs may not always be followed
  • Recommended ride-hailing apps:
    • Cars: Grab, InDrive, Green GSM
    • Motorcycle taxis: Angkas, Move It, JoyRide
  • Expect heavy traffic during peak hours: 7–10 AM and 4–8 PM
  • Cross streets safely:
    • Use pedestrian lanes
    • Ensure vehicles have fully stopped before crossing
    • Be vigilant and keep your belongings secure, especially in busy areas

🚗 Transportation

  • Public Transit Options:
    • From NAIA to DLSU: Detailed routes available. View the route here: Public Transport Directions
    • From Taft Area:
      • Take the LRT-1 (Light Rail Transit) and get off at Vito Cruz Station
      • Alternatively, take buses or jeepneys along Taft Avenue that pass near the university
  • Ride-Hailing (Recommended):
    • Cars: Grab, Green GSM, InDrive
    • Motorcycle taxis: Angkas, Move It, JoyRide
  • Taxi Advisory: Some drivers may attempt unmetered fares

🧳Helpful Tips for Travelers

  • Ways to Get Mobile Data (SIM & eSIM):
    • Airport Pick-Up Physical SIM (Klook – Globe 5G)
      • Pre-book a Globe prepaid SIM via Klook and pick it up at the airport upon arrival
      • Staff usually assist with activation and SIM registration using your passport
      • Best if you want a local number and help setting it up
    • Digital eSIM (Klook – Globe 5G eSIM)
      • For eSIM-compatible phones; receive a QR code for instant activation
      • No physical SIM needed
      • Best for convenience and avoiding airport lines
    • Notes:
      • Passport is required for SIM registration
      • Phone must be unlocked
      • eSIMs are ideal for short stays and data-only use (maps, Grab, messaging)
  • Nearby Medical & Assistance Options:
    • St. Luke's Medical Center Extension Clinic – ~10–15 minutes away
    • ManilaMed – ~10 minutes from Taft Avenue
  • Nearby Police & Security:
    • Malate Police Station
    • Pasay City Police Station
  • Emergency Hotlines in the Philippines:
    • Police / Fire / Ambulance: 911 (nationwide unified emergency number)
  • Traveler-Friendly Tips:
    • Keep your belongings with you at all times
    • Stick to well-lit, busy streets when walking from Vito Cruz Station to campus
    • Explore the area—cafes, shops, and local sights make it fun and generally safe
