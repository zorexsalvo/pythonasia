üóíÔ∏è Road Awareness Tips for Foreign Attendees

Exploring Quezon City and the Philippines can be an exciting experience, and we want to ensure you feel confident getting around. Here are some friendly tips to help:

  • Traffic Culture is Unique: Drivers here have their own rhythm, and traffic rules like stop signs and pedestrian lanes might not always be followed. Stay alert, and you'll soon get the hang of it.
  • Ride-Hailing for Convenience: Apps like Grab or Angkas are popular and reliable for getting around comfortably and hassle-free.
  • Plan for Traffic: The roads can get busy, especially during peak hours, so leaving a little earlier than planned can make your day smoother.
  • Cross Streets Safely: Use designated pedestrian crossings and make sure vehicles have stopped before stepping onto the road‚Äîbetter safe than sorry!

üöó Transportation

Ateneo de Manila University is accessible from the Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA):

  • Public Transportation: Detailed routes are available to guide attendees from NAIA to the university using public transit. View the route here: Public Transport Directions.
  • Ride-Hailing Services: For a more convenient option, attendees can use Grab, a widely used ride-hailing app in the Philippines. There's also a go-to ride-hailing app for moto-taxis like Move It and Angkas.
  • Caution: Some taxi drivers may attempt to charge unmetered fares to foreigners. If you prefer a set fare, we suggest using Grab, which provides transparent pricing.
